The Basic Code of Conduct

To ensure the prosperity, fairness, and mutual respect upon which our society is built, this Basic Code of Conduct is established as the foundational law of the land. Every citizen of Valerock, regardless of rank or guild, is bound by these articles. This is a living document, to be ratified and amended by the Council and the people as our society evolves.

Article I: The Sanctity of Property

  • A citizen's personal property, including their designated living quarters and personal storage, is inviolable. Accessing or taking items from another citizen's storage without their express permission is considered theft.
  • The property of a Guild, including its workshops, stockpiles, and equipment, is for official Guild use only. Unauthorized removal of items from a Guild stockpile is considered theft from the community.
  • The willful destruction or defacement of another citizen's property or any community building (griefing) is a severe offense.

Article II: The Principle of Fair Share

  • Resources designated as communal are to be taken in reasonable measure. Intentional hoarding to the detriment of others is forbidden.
  • The hoarding of tools distributed by Guild Wardens or the misrepresentation of need to acquire excess supply is a punishable offense.

Article III: The Law of the Land

  • The construction of personal quarters may only be done on land officially designated by the Builder's Guild and the Council.
  • Unauthorized modification of the landscape in a way that endangers or significantly inconveniences the community is forbidden.

Article IV: The Mandate of Civility

  • All citizens are expected to interact with one another with a baseline of respect. Harassment, threats, and intentionally disruptive behavior will not be tolerated.
  • Disagreements are to be handled through civil discourse or official mediation, not personal attacks.

Article V: The Oath of the Guildsman

  • Acceptance into a Guild constitutes an oath to perform one's duties to the best of their ability for the good of the community.
  • Consistent and willful negligence of one's duties is an offense against one's Guild and the community that relies upon it.

Article VI: The Supremacy of the Compact

  • Attempting to circumvent the systems of governance outlined in the Valerock Compact through exploits, cheating, or other forms of bad-faith manipulation is an offense against the entire society.

Judicial Procedure

Intra-Guild Offenses: Violations occurring within a single guild fall under the initial jurisdiction of the Guild's Warden.

Inter-Guild Offenses: Violations crossing guild lines must be immediately escalated to the Council of Wardens for arbitration. These cases shall be given the highest priority.
